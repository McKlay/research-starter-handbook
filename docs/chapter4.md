Journals are the traditional form of academic publication. They are:

- **Highly reviewed** (multiple reviewers, revisions, often several months)
- **Archival** (your final version becomes part of the permanent academic record)
- **Often longer-form** (more space for experiments, theory, background)

Journal articles are often considered the gold standard in many fields. The review process is rigorous, with multiple rounds of feedback and revision. This means your work will be scrutinized for clarity, originality, and impact. Journals also tend to have higher standards for experimental design and theoretical depth. If you want your research to stand the test of time, a journal is a strong choice.

Journals are ideal when:

- You want to present a *mature*, deeply polished piece of work
- You aim for long-term academic recognition
- Your university requires a journal-based thesis output

Some journals are open access, meaning anyone can read your work for free. Others are behind paywalls, which can limit visibility. Consider your audience and goals when choosing where to submit.
