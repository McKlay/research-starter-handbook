**ðŸ”¹ What Goes Into Each Section?**

### **Abstract**
- A summary of your entire paper in ~150â€“250 words  
- Should answer: What problem? What method? What result? Why it matters?

Write the abstract last, but keep notes as you go. A strong abstract is concise, specific, and highlights your main contribution.

### **Introduction**
- Hook the reader: Why is this problem important?  
- Define the gap in prior work  
- State your objective and main contribution clearly

Use the introduction to set the stage for your research. Make it clear why your work matters and what makes it different from previous efforts.

### **Related Work**
- Summarize key prior studies and how your work builds upon or differs from them  
- Use citations strategically to position your work

Organize this section thematically or by method, and always explain how your work fits into or advances the field.

### **Methodology**
- Describe your approach, system design, dataset, experimental setup  
- Be clear enough that someone could reproduce your work

Include enough detail for others to replicate your results. Use diagrams or pseudocode if it helps clarify your process.

### **Results**
- Show your findings with tables, graphs, metrics  
- Compare against baselines or prior work when possible

Highlight the most important findings and explain what they mean. Use visuals to make your results easy to interpret.

### **Conclusion**
- Restate what you achieved  
- Mention limitations and suggest future work  
- End with a strong takeaway message

The conclusion should leave readers with a clear sense of your contribution and its significance. Suggest next steps or open questions for future research.
